War Hero Cats

Throughout military history, cats have served aboard ships and in military installations, providing companionship, pest control, and boosting morale. Some rose to legendary status through acts of bravery and resilience.

Simon of HMS Amethyst

The Only Cat to Receive the Dickin Medal

Simon (c. 1947-1949) was a ship's cat who served on the Royal Navy sloop HMS Amethyst during the Yangtze Incident in 1949. When the ship was attacked by Chinese Communist forces, Simon was seriously wounded by shrapnel but survived. Despite his injuries, he continued to boost crew morale and kill rats that threatened the ship's food supplies during their months-long standoff.

Simon was awarded the PDSA Dickin Medal - often called the "Animal Victoria Cross" - becoming the only cat in history to receive this honor while on active duty. He was also given the Amethyst campaign ribbon and promoted to Able Seacat. Sadly, he died in quarantine shortly after returning to Britain, and thousands mourned his passing.

Dickin Medal Recipient Royal Navy 1949

Unsinkable Sam (Oscar)

Survived Three WWII Shipwrecks

Unsinkable Sam, originally named Oscar, is legendary for allegedly surviving the sinking of three ships during World War II. He reportedly began his naval career aboard the German battleship Bismarck, which was sunk in May 1941 with the loss of over 2,000 crew members.

Rescued by the British destroyer HMS Cossack, Sam survived when that ship was also torpedoed in October 1941. He was then transferred to HMS Ark Royal - which was itself torpedoed and sank in November 1941. After this third sinking, Sam was retired to a seaman's home in Belfast where he lived until 1955.

A pastel portrait titled "Oscar, the Bismarck's Cat" by Georgina Shaw-Baker is held by the National Maritime Museum in Greenwich.

3 Shipwrecks Survived WWII 1941

Mrs Chippy

Ship's Cat of Shackleton's Endurance

Mrs Chippy was a tabby cat who accompanied Sir Ernest Shackleton's Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition (1914-1917) aboard the ship Endurance. Despite the name, Mrs Chippy was actually male - he earned the name by following carpenter Harry "Chippy" McNish everywhere "like an overly attentive wife."

Mrs Chippy was known for his adventurous spirit, once falling overboard and surviving in the icy Antarctic waters until the ship could turn around. Expedition photographer Frank Hurley captured the only known photograph of Mrs Chippy perched on the shoulder of stowaway Perce Blackborow.

Tragically, when the Endurance became trapped in pack ice, Shackleton ordered Mrs Chippy to be shot, believing the cat could not survive the journey ahead. McNish never forgave Shackleton. In 2004, a bronze statue of Mrs Chippy was placed on McNish's grave in New Zealand.

Antarctic Explorer 1914-1915 Endurance Expedition

First Cat in Space (1963)

On October 18, 1963, a Parisian stray cat named F√©licette became the first and only cat to travel to space. Launched by the French space program from a site in the Algerian Sahara Desert, she reached an altitude of 157 kilometers (97 miles) during her 13-minute sub-orbital flight.

F√©licette was selected from 14 female cats who underwent training that included exposure to compression chambers and centrifuges. She was chosen partly for her calm temperament. Electrodes implanted in her brain transmitted neurological data back to Earth throughout the flight.

F√©licette successfully survived the flight and her capsule parachuted safely back to Earth. However, she was euthanized two months later so scientists could study her brain. For decades, she was largely forgotten, with credit often mistakenly given to a male cat named "Felix." In 2019, a bronze statue of F√©licette was unveiled at the International Space University in Strasbourg, France, funded by a Kickstarter campaign.

Political & Government Cats

Larry

Chief Mouser to the Cabinet Office, UK

Larry is a brown and white tabby cat who has served as Chief Mouser to the Cabinet Office at 10 Downing Street since February 2011. He was the first cat to be given this official government title in the modern era, complete with his own official biography on the gov.uk website.

Adopted from Battersea Dogs & Cats Home by then-Prime Minister David Cameron, Larry has now served under six Prime Ministers: Cameron, May, Johnson, Truss, Sunak, and Starmer. His official duties include "greeting guests to the house, inspecting security defences and testing antique furniture for napping quality."

Larry has become a media sensation, with over 900,000 followers on his parody X (Twitter) account @Number10cat. He has famously "photobombed" diplomatic meetings and is known for his territorial disputes with Palmerston, the Foreign Office cat.

Chief Mouser 10 Downing Street Since 2011

Stubbs

Honorary Mayor of Talkeetna, Alaska (1997-2017)

Stubbs (1997-2017) was a ginger cat who served as the honorary mayor of Talkeetna, Alaska for 20 years. Since Talkeetna is unincorporated and has no actual mayor, residents elected the tailless cat as their symbolic leader in 1997 when he was just a kitten found in a box of strays.

Stubbs became a major tourist attraction, drawing 30-40 visitors daily to Nagley's General Store where he "held office." His daily routine included drinking water mixed with catnip from a wine glass at a local restaurant. He survived numerous adventures, including falling into a restaurant fryer (thankfully turned off), being shot with a BB gun, and a 2013 dog attack that required emergency surgery.

After Stubbs passed away in 2017, the tradition continued with twin cats Aurora and Denali named as honorary co-mayors.

Honorary Mayor Alaska 20 Years in Office

Canadian Famous Cats

Toronto's Contribution to Cat History

Did you know that one of the world's most distinctive cat breeds - the Sphynx - was born right here in Toronto? The city has a special place in feline history!

Prune - The Original Sphynx

Father of the Sphynx Breed, Born in Toronto (1966)

In 1966, a hairless kitten named Prune was born in the Roncesvalles neighborhood of Toronto to a regular domestic shorthair cat named Elizabeth. This genetic mutation would eventually lead to the development of the Sphynx breed, one of the most recognizable cat breeds in the world.

Riyadh Bawa, a University of Toronto science student, recognized the hairlessness as a recessive genetic trait. Working with his mother Yania (an experienced Siamese breeder) and breeders Keese and Rita Tenhove, they began developing what they initially called "Canadian Hairless" cats.

While Prune's direct bloodline eventually died out, his birth sparked the breeding programs that led to today's Sphynx cats. Modern Sphynx trace their origins to a second Canadian line and cats from Minnesota, but Toronto's Prune will forever be remembered as the cat who started it all.

Toronto Born Sphynx Founder 1966

Smudge

The Ottawa Meme Cat ("Woman Yelling at Cat")

Smudge is a white cat from Ottawa who became one half of one of the most famous memes of the 2010s: "Woman Yelling at Cat." The meme combines a still of Taylor Armstrong crying from The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills with a photo of Smudge sitting at a dinner table looking confused in front of a salad.

The original photo of Smudge was posted by his owner Miranda on Tumblr in 2018. The cat's confused expression, combined with his apparent disdain for vegetables, struck a chord with internet users worldwide. The meme has been used billions of times to express disagreement, confusion, or frustration.

Smudge continues to live in Ottawa and has his own Instagram account with over 1.4 million followers.

Ottawa, Canada Viral Meme 2019

Record-Breaking Cats

Creme Puff

Oldest Cat Ever - 38 Years and 3 Days

Creme Puff (August 3, 1967 - August 6, 2005) holds the Guinness World Record as the oldest cat ever verified. She lived an astonishing 38 years and 3 days in Austin, Texas with her owner Jake Perry.

What was her secret? According to Perry, Creme Puff enjoyed an unconventional diet that included dry cat food supplemented with broccoli, eggs, turkey bacon, coffee with cream, and "an eyedropper full of red wine" every few days. Perry also kept her active with daily play and built cat-friendly features throughout his home including climbing steps on the walls.

Remarkably, Perry also owned the previous record holder, Grandpa Rex Allen, who lived to 34 years. In cat years, Creme Puff's 38 years would be equivalent to approximately 169 human years.

World Record 38 Years Old Austin, Texas

Blackie

World's Wealthiest Cat - $12.5 Million

Blackie holds the Guinness World Record as the wealthiest cat ever. When British antique dealer Ben Rea died in 1988, he left his entire fortune of approximately 7 million pounds (around $12.5 million USD) to Blackie, the last surviving of the 15 cats he shared his mansion with.

Rea refused to acknowledge his family in his will, instead dividing his fortune among three cat charities with the stipulation that they care for Blackie. The charities - Cats Protection, the Royal Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals, and other animal welfare organizations - used the funds to ensure Blackie lived out his days in luxury.

$12.5 Million World's Richest Cat UK

Internet Famous Cats

Grumpy Cat (Tardar Sauce)

Internet Icon Worth Millions

Tardar Sauce (April 4, 2012 - May 14, 2019), better known as Grumpy Cat, became one of the most famous cats in internet history. Her permanently "grumpy" expression was caused by feline dwarfism and an underbite, giving her a perpetually displeased appearance that spawned millions of memes.

After her photo was posted to Reddit in September 2012 by her owner's brother, Grumpy Cat quickly became a viral sensation. Her fame led to a best-selling book, a Lifetime movie ("Grumpy Cat's Worst Christmas Ever"), appearances on major TV shows, and her own line of merchandise including Grumppuccino iced coffee.

At her peak, Grumpy Cat was estimated to be worth over $100 million, and her owners successfully defended her brand in court, winning $710,000 in a trademark infringement case in 2018. Her death in 2019 was mourned worldwide.

Viral Meme $100M+ Value 2012-2019

Nala Cat

Guinness World Record - Most Instagram Followers for a Cat

Nala is a Siamese/tabby mix who holds the Guinness World Record for most Instagram followers for a cat, with over 4.3 million followers as of 2020. She was adopted from a shelter in Los Angeles at just four months old by her owner Varisiri Methachittiphan.

Named after Simba's friend in The Lion King, Nala captured hearts with her striking blue eyes and adorable expressions. Her Instagram account started in 2012 simply to share photos with family in Thailand, but her cartoon-like appearance quickly attracted a massive following.

Nala's success has been leveraged for good - she has her own cat food brand "Love Nala" and actively promotes animal shelter adoption. She was named to Forbes' list of top pet influencers and continues to use her platform to advocate for rescue animals.

4.3M+ Followers Guinness Record Shelter Cat

Maru

YouTube's Most Watched Cat

Maru is a Scottish Fold cat from Japan who became famous for his love of boxes. His YouTube channel, started in 2007, has accumulated over 500 million views, making him one of the most-watched cats in internet history. He held the Guinness World Record for most YouTube video views for an individual animal.

Maru's appeal lies in his simple joy: he will attempt to fit into any box, bag, or container, no matter how small. His determination to squeeze into impossibly tiny spaces, combined with his round face and chunky body, has made him beloved worldwide. His owner, known only as "mugumogu," has published several books about him in Japan.

500M+ Views Japan Box Enthusiast

Working Cats

Tama

Station Master Cat of Kishi Station, Japan

Tama (1999-2015) was a calico cat who served as the official station master of Kishi Station on the Kishigawa Line in Wakayama Prefecture, Japan. She was "hired" in 2007 when the struggling railway line was looking for ways to attract visitors, and she exceeded all expectations.

Tama's presence brought in an estimated 1.1 billion yen ($11 million) annually in tourism revenue, saving the railway from potential closure. She was paid in cat food and was promoted multiple times, eventually becoming "Super Station Master" and "Operating Officer" of the railway company.

When Tama died in 2015, she was given a Shinto funeral and was posthumously elevated to the status of goddess. The station was redesigned in her honor with a cat-shaped roof, and her assistant Nitama (Tama II) succeeded her as station master.

Station Master $11M Tourism Japan

Dewey Readmore Books

World's Most Famous Library Cat

Dewey Readmore Books (1987-2006) was an orange tabby cat who lived at the Spencer Public Library in Spencer, Iowa for 19 years. Found as a kitten in the library's book drop on a frigid January night in 1988, he was adopted by the library and became a beloved community figure.

Dewey's story was told in the bestselling book "Dewey: The Small-Town Library Cat Who Touched the World" by library director Vicki Myron, which was translated into 34 languages and sold millions of copies worldwide. The book inspired people globally and led to increased attention on library cats everywhere.

Dewey was known for greeting visitors at the door, sitting on patrons' laps while they read, and bringing joy to a struggling farming community during difficult economic times. His funeral was attended by hundreds of mourners from around the world.

Library Cat Iowa Best-Selling Book

Room 8

The Most Famous School Cat in America

Room 8 (c. 1947-1968) was a gray tabby cat who appeared at Elysian Heights Elementary School in Los Angeles in 1952 and became the school's unofficial mascot for 16 years. Every school day, he would arrive in the morning and leave in the afternoon, spending summers elsewhere - a schedule he maintained perfectly for over a decade.

Room 8 received fan mail from around the world, inspired a documentary film, and was featured in Look Magazine and on CBS News. His routine of showing up only during school hours captured the imagination of children everywhere. When he died in 1968, his obituary appeared in the Los Angeles Times, and he was buried in the L.A. Pet Memorial Park with a headstone that reads "Room 8, 1947-1968."

School Cat Los Angeles 16 Years

Historical & Literary Cats

Hodge

Samuel Johnson's Beloved Cat

Hodge (d. 1784) was the cat of Dr. Samuel Johnson, the famous English writer who compiled the first comprehensive English dictionary. Johnson's devotion to Hodge was so notable that his biographer James Boswell specifically recorded it, writing that Johnson "frequently went out himself to buy oysters" for Hodge rather than sending his servants, lest they resent the cat.

Johnson once declared Hodge "a very fine cat indeed" - high praise from the man known for his sharp tongue. Today, a bronze statue of Hodge sits outside Johnson's house at 17 Gough Square in London, depicting the cat sitting contentedly on a copy of Johnson's dictionary with oyster shells at his feet.

18th Century London Dictionary Cat

Trim

The Circumnavigator Cat

Trim (c. 1799-1804) was a ship's cat who accompanied explorer Matthew Flinders on his voyages, including the first circumnavigation of Australia. Born aboard HMS Reliance, Trim proved his worth by falling overboard and climbing back up a rope to rejoin the ship - an act that earned him Flinders' lifelong admiration.

Flinders was so devoted to Trim that he wrote a biographical tribute to the cat, describing him as "one of the finest animals I ever saw" with "an air of superiority." Trim accompanied Flinders on the Investigator during the circumnavigation of Australia and was with him when he was imprisoned by the French on Mauritius. Sadly, Trim disappeared there, likely killed for food during a period of scarcity.

Today, a bronze statue of Trim sits near a statue of Flinders in Sydney, and another was placed in the window of the house in Mauritius where Flinders was imprisoned.

Circumnavigated Australia Explorer 1799-1804

All Ball

Koko the Gorilla's Beloved Kitten

All Ball was a gray Manx kitten adopted in 1984 by Koko, the famous gorilla who learned American Sign Language. Koko had long asked her handlers for a cat, and when she was finally allowed to choose a kitten from an abandoned litter, she selected All Ball and named her using sign language.

Koko treated All Ball with remarkable gentleness, cradling the kitten like a baby gorilla. When All Ball was tragically killed by a car just months later, Koko's grief demonstrated the depth of emotional connection possible between species. She signed "Bad, sad, bad" and "Frown, cry, frown, sad" when told of All Ball's death.

Koko later adopted more cats, including Lipstick and Smoky, but All Ball remains the most famous for what she revealed about interspecies love and communication.

Koko's Cat 1984 Interspecies Bond

Orangey

Hollywood's Most Decorated Cat Actor

Orangey (c. 1951-1965) was an orange tabby cat who became Hollywood's most successful feline actor. He is best known for playing "Cat" opposite Audrey Hepburn in Breakfast at Tiffany's (1961), one of the most iconic cat roles in film history.

Orangey won two PATSY Awards (the animal equivalent of an Oscar) - for Rhubarb (1951) and Breakfast at Tiffany's - making him the only cat to win the award twice. He also appeared in Gigot with Jackie Gleason and in episodes of various TV series.

Despite his on-screen charm, Orangey was notoriously difficult to work with. He was known for scratching actors and disappearing during filming. Animal trainer Frank Inn was the only person who could consistently work with him.

2 PATSY Awards Breakfast at Tiffany's Hollywood

The Sacred Cats of Ancient Egypt

Worshipped as Gods for 3,000 Years

No discussion of famous cats would be complete without acknowledging the cats of ancient Egypt, who enjoyed a status unparalleled in human history. The goddess Bastet, originally depicted as a fierce lioness, evolved into a cat-headed deity representing home, fertility, and protection.

Egyptian cats were so revered that killing one, even accidentally, was punishable by death. When a household cat died, family members would shave their eyebrows in mourning. Millions of cats were mummified and buried with the same care given to humans - so many that Victorian-era entrepreneurs actually ground them up for fertilizer.

The export of cats from Egypt was forbidden for centuries, though they eventually spread throughout the Mediterranean through smuggling. These Egyptian cats are the ancestors of many domestic cats alive today.
