What is CFMG Certification?

The Certified Feline Master Groomer (CFMG) credential represents the highest level of professional cat grooming certification. Here's what it means for you and your cat.

Understanding CFMG Certification

When you see the letters CFMG after a cat groomer's name, you're looking at the highest credential in professional feline grooming. The Certified Feline Master Groomer certification is awarded by the National Cat Groomers Institute of America (NCGI), the premier organization dedicated to establishing and maintaining standards for cat grooming professionals.

Unlike dog grooming certifications, which are common and varied, cat-specific certifications are relatively rare. Cats have unique anatomical, behavioral, and handling requirements that differ significantly from dogs. A CFMG groomer has demonstrated mastery of these specialized skills through rigorous training and examination.

Why Cat-Specific Certification Matters

Many pet groomers are trained primarily in dog grooming and simply adapt those techniques for cats. However, cats are not small dogs‚Äîthey have different skin, coat structures, temperaments, and stress responses. CFMG certification ensures your groomer understands these differences and has been specifically trained in feline-appropriate techniques.

The National Cat Groomers Institute of America (NCGI)

The National Cat Groomers Institute of America was founded with a singular mission: to establish professional standards for cat grooming and provide training that focuses exclusively on feline care. Located in Greenville, South Carolina, NCGI offers both in-person and online training programs for aspiring cat groomers.

NCGI's Mission

  • Establish industry standards for professional cat grooming
  • Provide comprehensive education on feline anatomy, behavior, and grooming techniques
  • Promote safe, humane handling practices for cats
  • Certify groomers who demonstrate mastery of cat-specific skills
  • Educate the public on the importance of professional cat grooming

You can learn more about NCGI at their official website: nationalcatgroomers.com

The CFMG Examination Process

Earning a CFMG certification is no easy feat. Candidates must demonstrate their knowledge and skills through a rigorous examination process:

Written Examination

A comprehensive written test covering:

  • Feline anatomy and physiology
  • Coat types and grooming requirements
  • Skin conditions and health concerns
  • Safe handling and restraint techniques
  • Tool selection and maintenance
  • Business practices and client communication

Practical Examination

The hands-on portion requires candidates to demonstrate:

  • Full groom on a long-haired cat - Including bathing, drying, de-matting, and styling
  • Full groom on a short-haired cat - Demonstrating different techniques
  • Handling skills - Working with cats of varying temperaments
  • Time management - Completing grooms efficiently without rushing
  • Safety protocols - Maintaining a safe environment throughout

Continuing Education

CFMG certification isn't a one-time achievement. Certified groomers are encouraged to pursue continuing education to stay current with the latest techniques, products, and research in feline care. This commitment to ongoing learning ensures CFMG groomers maintain the highest standards throughout their careers.

How to Verify CFMG Certification

Legitimate CFMG groomers are proud of their certification and happy to discuss their credentials. You can:

  1. Ask to see their certificate - NCGI provides official certificates to certified groomers
  2. Contact NCGI directly - The institute can verify certification status
  3. Look for displayed credentials - Many CFMG groomers display their certification prominently
  4. Check their website - Look for mentions of NCGI training and certification

  • Programs offered: Cat grooming certification courses, continuing education, online training
